Manage-Mint Express - Done

For my Junior year in college, I took an Advanced Object Oriented Programming class, which covered all of the advanced OO concepts, like polymorphism, inheritence, abstraction, encapsulation, etc. For this class, we could take a final exam, or tie all the concepts we learned together into one big project, and that's what I chose to do. I made a small software package called "Manage-Mint Express" (which, I'm not sold) on the name, but it served as a little-league/rec-league baseball team manager, with stat tracking and such.

I plan to, eventually, convert this to a web app and potentially sell it to local teams, and offer to maintain it for a year in the package. I think converting it to a webapp will allow me to have a bit of a bigger range of options for the UI than than Java's Swing library does. Plus it would allow it to be used in the field, rather than just in an office.

This website - In Progress

This website is my first dip into the web dev field. I am a more traditional back end dev, and beyond that, I love making command line tools, so web dev isn't someting that I would traditionally see myself doing, but I want a portfolio website, so I've decided to learn how to use HTML, CSS, and JS, in an attempt to not just brute force my way through, but rather actually learn the craft as a whole.

This site is a massive work in progress, and shouldn't ever be viewed as "done." Even if the site is visually done and dusted, it's important to know that I am always learning and updating this site, both in terms of the content and in terms of techniques and styles.
